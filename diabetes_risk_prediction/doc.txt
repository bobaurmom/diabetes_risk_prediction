Polyuria: Excessive or frequent urination. When blood sugar is high, the kidneys work overtime to filter it out, pulling water along with it.

Polydipsia: Excessive thirst. This is usually a direct result of polyuria; your body is trying to replace the fluids lost through frequent urination.

Polyphagia: Excessive hunger. Even if you're eating, your cells aren't getting the energy (glucose) they need because insulin isn't working correctly, leaving you feeling starved.

Sudden weight loss: When the body can't use glucose for energy, it starts burning fat and muscle for fuel instead.

Weakness: A general lack of energy because the body's "fuel" (sugar) is staying in the bloodstream instead of entering the cells.

Obesity: A significant risk factor for Type 2 diabetes. Excess body fat can make cells more resistant to insulin.

Alopecia: Hair loss. This can be caused by the physical stress of a chronic illness or poor circulation affecting hair follicles.

Visual blurring: High blood sugar can cause the lens of the eye to swell, changing your ability to focus.

Itching & Genital thrush: High sugar levels in sweat and urine provide a "buffet" for yeast and bacteria, leading to frequent infections (like thrush) and itchy skin.

Delayed healing: High glucose levels can impair white blood cell function and decrease circulation, making it harder for the body to repair cuts or sores.

Irritability: Fluctuating blood sugar levels can have a direct impact on mood and mental clarity.

Partial paresis: Weakness or partial loss of voluntary movement, often due to nerve damage (neuropathy) caused by prolonged high blood sugar.

Muscle stiffness: High sugar levels can affect the collagen in tendons and joints, leading to decreased range of motion.
